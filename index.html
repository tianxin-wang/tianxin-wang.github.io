About Me

I am a PhD student in the Integrated Program in Cellular and Biomedical Sciences at Columbia University Irving Medical Center, specializing in Systems Biology under the advisement of Dr. Andrea Califano.


Prior to joining Columbia, I earned my B.S. in Biological Sciences with a minor in Computer Science from the University of Chicago, where I conducted research with Dr. Tao Pan and Dr. Matthias Steinruecken.


My research lies at the intersection of wet-lab experimentation and computational analysis. I am passionate about utilizing functional genomics, high-dimensional statistics, and probabilistic modeling to understand tumor heterogeneity and gene regulatory networks.

Selected Research

Protein Activity-based Single-cell Analysis in DSRCT Columbia University (Califano Lab)

Conducting integrative analysis of single-cell RNA sequencing datasets from Desmoplastic Small Round Cell Tumor (DSRCT) patients. Utilizing deep generative modeling (scVI) and ARACNe/VIPER to infer gene regulatory networks and identify shared tumor sub-states.

Engineering Mischarged Transfer RNAs University of Chicago (Tao Pan Lab)

Facilitated the development of engineered missense-correcting tRNA (mc-tRNA) as potential therapeutics for pathogenic mutations. Investigated whether cis-regulatory elements exist around tRNA genes via truncation experiments.
